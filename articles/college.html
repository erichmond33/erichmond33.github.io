Stop caring about your degree: How I look at college

For most people, a degree represents the ability to follow instructions. They believe that trading their mission for a meaningless job, security, and money will make them happy.

I disagree.

Entire storage units of crap don't make you happy, and the security is a lie. Most of all, nothing is worth giving up your mission.

My college years should give me a higher chance to do work that is important to me. However, the degree is nearly worthless.

Today all desirable companies - ones with a purpose you believe in - ask the same thing. What problems have you solved, what things have you built, or what teams have you led? Only having a college degree leaves you with three answers. None, nothing, or I'm sorry for wasting your time.

But these questions are fantastic guidance whether you want to work for a company or start your own thing. Unless you are fired up to follow instructions, spend as much effort and time on those three things as possible.

This isn't a burden either. I don't dread starting a new project. It isn't extra homework that I have to do.

It is a chance to be useful, connect, and touch the hearts of people around me today.
It is making something that I love (and hopefully others too.)
It is fixing things that are ugly and broken.
It is passion not stress.

I think we all want that, and it might be the most important thing.

Spending as much time as I can on these things is so important to me that I don't care about my grades. I do good enough to pass and keep my scholarships. But the world barley cares about my degree, and so do I.

I treat my classes as introductions to new ideas - not vital information for the test. I use this new information and extra time to better fulfill my mission.

I would spend as much time as it takes to find yours.
