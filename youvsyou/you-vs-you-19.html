On Gratitude...

Gratitude is one of the most underrated virtues in both its value from a zoomed-out mindset or outlook perspective and from a pragmatic zoomed-in day-to-day emotional grounding perspective. Being grateful from the zoomed-out view encourages us to take the opportunities we are given, no matter how big or how small, very seriously and treat them with care and effort. Being grateful from the zoomed-in perspective is fantastic for bringing about peace and happiness within ourselves emotionally right now ‚Äì it is hard to be both genuinely grateful for every little thing in your life and be upset at the same time. Try it now?

Article on Understanding Feminization in the Workplace üíº
The Great Feminization by Helen Andrews

This read caught me off guard with how important and spot-on it is. I found it useful for two reasons. One, women approach conflict and competition wayyy different then men do: "In short, men wage conflict openly while women covertly undermine or ostracize their enemies." This is a big deal. Two, for me at least, this overall feminization explains why a lot of office jobs feel so sad...? The feminized office feels very... sterile? It feels to me that feminized work culture prizes looking productive and avoiding friction more than pursuing deep, difficult, meaningful outcomes. The target feels slightly misaligned from what makes work feel purposeful.
