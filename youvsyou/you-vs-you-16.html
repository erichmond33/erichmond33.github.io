On Modern Architecture...

Why is every building so bland and ugly these days? Every city looks exactly the same. Architecture used to be so beautiful and ornate. This is such an interesting phenomena, and I think there is a great answer but an even more important byproduct.

Buildings are the way they are because mid-20th century zoning codes locked in modernist design principles, but these regulations make alternative building styles difficult or illegal to construct. However, modernism succeeded brilliantly at creating buildings that are dramatically safer and more efficient than before ‚Äì solving the problem of disease ridden slums and post-war housing shortages. Efficiency comes with a cost.

Modern architecture is built by no one for no one. Buildings don't welcome you or offer any spectacle. Most American cities look identical and encourage everyone not to have any vested interest in them. They don't want you care. And if you don't care about anything, you'll be much easier to enslave. Who is "They" you ask? "They" might be specific power hungry people. "They" might be the natural forces of chaos humans have been struggling with since forever.

Life has many such problems like this architecture situation. Nobody is quite sure why it is happening. It's kinda good but also kinda not so great and sad. It would be a monumental task to try and fix as a single individual. Yet despite these rigid systems, everything is always seemingly on the brink of collapse. The only answer is to Care ‚Äì Care with a capital "C". Care like your life and your children's lives depend on it because, although we may forget it, they do.
